LOGISTICS PACKING

Overview

  • Simulates a real-world automated logistics packing scenario.
  • Focuses on robotic picking from a moving conveyor system and packing into target containers.
  • Evaluates both efficiency and packing quality.

Task Description

  • 10 picking bins move along a conveyor belt at a controlled speed.
  • Each bin contains identical items.
  • Item properties (shape, size, weight) vary between different bins.
  • Each bin is available for picking only within a limited time window.

Packing Variants

  • Standard Version: Uses a rigid plastic container as the packing bin.
  • Advanced Version: Uses a cardboard box and requires proper box closure after packing.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Total picking time for all 10 items.
  • Total packing time for all 10 placements.
  • Neat and tight stacking of items.
  • Compliance with container height and closure requirements.

LAB EXPERIMENTS

Overview

  • Focuses on robotic manipulation in life science R&D laboratory environments.
  • Emphasizes precision, stability, and careful handling of delicate equipment.

Stage 1: Column Installation

  • Identify the correct silica gel column using the robot's vision system.
  • Align and tighten the Luer lock connection with high precision.
  • Mount the column securely on a fixed rack.
  • Ensure a leak-free and stable connection.

Stage 2: Sample Tube Organization

  • Transfer glass sample tubes from a random arrangement to an organized layout.
  • Fill all 30 positions on the target rack.
  • Arrange tubes into a precise 5 Ã— 6 grid.

Stage 3: Selective Sample Processing

  • Select and transfer specified sample tubes according to a given diagram.
  • Precisely pour remaining liquid samples into a waste container.
  • Avoid spillage and ensure accurate liquid handling.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Column alignment and tightening accuracy.
  • Tube placement precision.
  • Sample selection correctness.
  • Liquid handling stability and control.
  • Task completion time and overall system reliability.

DEFORMABLE MANIPULATION

Overview

  • Evaluates robotic manipulation of deformable objects in manufacturing and service scenarios.
  • Uses a T-shirt as the representative flexible object.

Task Description

  • Load a T-shirt into the workspace.
  • Unload the T-shirt after manipulation.
  • Fold the T-shirt into a neat and consistent configuration.

Core Skills Assessed

  • Perception and tracking of deformable objects.
  • Compliant grasping and manipulation.
  • Control of object deformation and alignment.
  • Consistency and quality of the final folded result.
